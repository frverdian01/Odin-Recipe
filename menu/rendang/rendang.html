RENDANG

What is rendang?

Rendang (/ˈrəndɑːŋ/ REN-DUNG; Indonesian pronunciation: [rənˈdaŋ]) is a Minang dish originating from the Minangkabau region in West Sumatra, Indonesia.[5] It has spread across Indonesian cuisine to the cuisines of neighbouring Southeast Asian countries such as Malaysia, Singapore, Brunei and the Philippines.[2] Rendang is often described as a rich dish of meat — most commonly beef (rendang daging) — that has been slow cooked and braised in a coconut milk seasoned with a herb and spice mixture,[6][7] until the liquids evaporate and the meat turns dark brown and tender, becoming caramelized and infused with rich flavours.

    Ingredient

  • Royco Bumbu Rempah Serbaguna 525g
  • Beef
  • Red Chili
  • Coconut Milk
  • Lime Leaves
  • Tumeric Leaf
  • Bay Leaf
  • Lemongrass

    Making Rendang

  1. Heat Oil. Put in red chili, turmeric leaves, bay leaves, lime leaves, and lemongrass.
  2. RAdd Royco Spice Base Rendang. Stir until well blended.
  3. Add beef. Stir until the meat changes colour.
  4. Add the coconut milk, mix well.
  5. After boiling and become kaleo, stir occasionally so as not to burn.
  6. Stir the rendang until it turns out the oil, the colour becomes red.
  7. Stir the rendang until the colour turns to black.
  8. Turn off the heat, lift, and serve.
